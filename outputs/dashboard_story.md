# Dashboard Story

## Executive Summary

The retail business is performing well overall, with approximately `$217.02M` in sales, `$33.31M` in profit, and an overall profit margin of `15.35%`. The dashboard shows that the strongest business performance is coming from Technology products, while the biggest risks are linked to Furniture returns and high-discount orders.

The dashboard connects five executive questions:

1. How is sales performance trending?
2. Which regions are strongest?
3. Which categories and sub-categories drive profit?
4. How does discounting affect profit?
5. Where are return risks concentrated?

## What Is Performing Well

Technology is the strongest category in the business. It contributes approximately `$153.90M` in sales and `$28.04M` in profit, with a profit margin of `18.22%`. The top sub-categories by profit are all Technology sub-categories, including Copiers, Accessories, Phones, and Machines.

Regional performance is also broadly healthy. South is the highest-sales region, with approximately `$64.69M` in sales and `$9.99M` in profit. East has a slightly higher profit margin than other regions, making it important from a profitability perspective even though its total sales are lower than South and North.

Customer segments are balanced in sales contribution. Home Office, Consumer, and Corporate each contribute around `$70M` or more in sales, so the business is not dependent on only one customer segment.

## What Is Underperforming

Furniture is the weakest major category from a profitability and return-risk perspective. It contributes approximately `$51.64M` in sales, but only `$3.56M` in profit. Its profit margin is approximately `6.89%`, far below Technology.

Furniture also has the highest category return rate at approximately `7.67%`. This makes it a priority area for management review because returns can reduce profit, increase operating cost, and damage customer experience.

High-discount orders are also underperforming on margin. Orders in the high-discount band have an average discount of approximately `27.85%` and a profit margin of only `7.45%`. This is much lower than the `20.56%` margin seen on no-discount orders.

## What Risks Are Visible

The first visible risk is discount erosion. As discount levels increase, profit margin falls. This means some promotions may be increasing sales volume while weakening profitability.

The second visible risk is Furniture returns. Furniture has both a lower margin and a higher return rate, which may indicate issues with product quality, customer expectations, delivery damage, or post-sale satisfaction.

The third visible risk is Home Office return behavior. Home Office is the highest-sales segment, but it also has the highest return rate at approximately `5.67%`. Since this segment is commercially important, the return risk should be investigated before it affects future growth.

The fourth visible risk is shipping performance. Standard Class has the longest average delivery time at approximately `4.71` days. Although its return rate is not the highest, delivery performance should still be monitored because it can affect customer ratings and repeat purchases.

## What Opportunities Are Visible

The strongest opportunity is to grow Technology sales while protecting margin. Technology already has high sales, high profit, and relatively low return risk.

Another opportunity is to learn from East's profitability. East has the highest regional profit margin, even though South has the highest sales. This may point to a better product mix, pricing pattern, or discount structure.

There is also an opportunity to improve Furniture performance. If leadership can reduce Furniture returns and improve margin, the category could contribute more profit without needing major sales growth.

Finally, discount optimization is a major opportunity. Reducing unnecessary high discounts could improve profit margin without requiring additional sales volume.

## Recommended Business Actions

1. Protect and grow Technology products because they are the strongest profit driver.
2. Review Furniture return reasons, especially for Bookcases, Furnishings, Chairs, and Tables.
3. Create discount guardrails for high-discount orders.
4. Compare East and South to understand why East has stronger margin while South has higher sales.
5. Analyze Home Office returns by category and sub-category.
6. Monitor Standard Class delivery performance because it carries the largest sales volume and longest average delivery time.
7. Use the dashboard filters to review region, category, and segment-level patterns before making operational decisions.

## Limitations Of The Dashboard

1. The dashboard is based only on the provided dataset for 2024 and 2025.
2. Return reasons are not available, so return causes must be inferred from patterns.
3. Customer acquisition cost, marketing spend, and inventory availability are not included.
4. The dashboard does not include competitor pricing or external market conditions.
5. Customer rating has some missing values, so it was not used as a primary KPI.
6. Campaign channel has some missing values and was not used as a main dashboard filter in the final executive layout.

## Suggested Next Analysis

1. Return root-cause analysis for Furniture.
2. Discount effectiveness analysis by category, region, and campaign channel.
3. Customer segment analysis for Home Office return behavior.
4. Shipping delay analysis by region, ship mode, and product category.
5. Product-level profitability analysis for Technology and Furniture.
6. Monthly seasonality analysis to identify recurring sales peaks and drops.

## Closing Story

The business is profitable and has a strong sales base, but profitability is not evenly distributed. Technology is carrying a large share of profit, while Furniture creates return and margin pressure. Regional performance is healthy, with South leading sales and East showing stronger margin. Discounting needs tighter control because high-discount orders show much weaker profitability. The recommended leadership focus is to scale profitable categories, control discount leakage, and reduce returns in the categories and customer segments where risk is highest.
